On the left is a scatterplot of 1000 stars, with ColorIndex on the x-axis and AbsMagnitude on the y-axis. You can change the variables using the dropdown menus. The amount of stars can be changed using the slider. If you click on a star you get scrolled down to two charts with data about that star. The currently selected star black. The colors of the stars are an approximation of the light which it emits. Color index is used to scale the data to all the visible light (a rainbow)

There are multiple colorindices, all of which are a ratio between two intensities of light. This colorindex is the ratio between blue light and visible light. Using the colorindex of a star astronomers can approximate the temperature of that star.

The absolute magnitude (AbsMagnitude) is a way for astronomers to compare different stars. It is a number to indicate the amount of light one detects at a distance of 10 parsec (3.09 * 10^14 km).
